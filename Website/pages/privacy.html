Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

Crisp Construction Ltd ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclosure, and safeguard your information when you visit our website.

1. What Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of personal information:

  • Contact information – such as your name, email address, phone number, and postal address when you contact us
  • Property information – details about properties you may be interested in or wish to sell
  • Technical data – such as IP address, browser type, and browsing behaviour through cookies (see Cookie Policy below)

2. Why We Collect It

We use your personal information for the following purposes:

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide the information or services you request
  • To communicate with you about properties, developments, and services that may interest you
  • To improve our website and user experience
  • To comply with legal obligations

3. Legal Basis

Under the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), we process your personal information based on:

  • Consent – where you have given us permission to contact you
  • Legitimate interests – where necessary for our business operations, such as responding to enquiries
  • Legal obligation – where we are required to process data by law

4. How Long We Keep It

We will only retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes we collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

Typically, we will keep enquiry data for up to 2 years after our last correspondence, unless you become a client, in which case we retain data for longer in line with our legal obligations.

5. Sharing Your Information

We may share your information with:

  • Third-party service providers who assist us in operating our website or conducting our business
  • Professional advisers, including lawyers, bankers, and insurers
  • Regulatory authorities, where required by law

We will not sell your personal information to third parties.

6. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Access – You can request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Correction – You can ask us to correct inaccurate personal data
  • Erasure – You can request deletion of your personal data (in certain circumstances)
  • Objection – You can object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Data portability – You can request your data in a portable format
  • Withdraw consent – You can withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below.

7. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to distinguish you from other users and to improve your browsing experience. When you first visit our site, you will be shown a cookie banner asking for your consent to use non-essential cookies.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by adjusting your browser settings or clearing stored cookies.

8. How to Complain

If you have any concerns about how we have handled your personal data, you have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):

  • Website: www.ico.org.uk
  • Address: Information Commissioner's Office, Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire SK9 5AF
  • Phone: 0303 123 1113
